Chimney.ai – Designing An Interactive Map To Find Homes Faster

Chimney, a property tech startup, wants to leverage all possible data points to help home buyers find their next home, fast. For our MVP, the team wanted to find a way to integrate a map as part of the property results page. However, it was unclear how buyers should be able to interact with the map.

How might we help buyers find property with more confidence and less effort with a map?  

There is so much data available that can be used to help buyers find their next home. The only problem is, there is too much of it to browse through, making the whole process exhausting. The team had ideas to integrate a map alongside property listings to help buyers situate themselves during their search. Though, could it be used for more than just that?

The challenge here was to help home buyeres make a decision with twice as much data and half the hassle using a map.


Early Insights

Being short on time for our MVP, we made assumptions about our home buyers’ search process and pain points. Chimney also had a need to use the map as a market differentiator, so I had to account for my stakeholders’s preliminary product vision and business drivers.

Design Process

For this project, I worked closely with our Product team to identify must haves, and our Engineering team to understand how expensive each map interaction would be.

Introducing Chimney’s Interactive Map Experience

The map is currently in Beta as of July 2023 to start testing with real users.

Impact

Though the new map is in Beta, we expect that the heat map will help provide buyers with a simple visual guide to find and decide on potential neighbourhoods and properties.

It also led to discussion about future design considerations to explore: How might we simplify the information on the map? (some info appears redundant) How does this interactivity translate to Mobile Web or a Native Mobile App?
